The manifesto

The swift spends its life in flight. It crosses borders without ever asking permission. Wherever it lands, it is home.

We grew up between two languages, two kitchens, two ways of saying "home". We know the look that asks where you're really from.

FOREIGNERS was born from that: a brand for those who come from somewhere else and no longer have to apologize for being here. Not a trend. A belonging.

FOW: Family Over Worries. It's embroidered on the sleeve, and stitched into the model: half of what we earn goes back to those who arrive, and to the cultures they bring with them.

50% Carrying culture forward.

This is not a marketing stunt or a symbolic percentage buried in fine print. On every piece sold, half of the profits go to organizations that support people who just arrived: language classes, paperwork, housing, a first job, and projects that keep their cultures alive.

This is bigger than clothing. It's a reminder that we are all human, whichever side of a border we were born on.

  • WherePartner organizations announced with every drop, chosen with the community.
  • ProofThe amount donated is published after every drop, down to the cent.
  • WhyBecause we arrived one day too, and someone reached out a hand.
