FAQ · Family Over Worries

Questions, answered.

Everything you might want to know before the first drop — the mission, the money, the bird.

The brand
What is FOREIGNERS?

A clothing brand built on one idea: every foreigner belongs here. Founded by foreigners, for foreigners — and for everyone who believes that no border decides someone's worth. Half of our profits go to organizations that support newcomers and the cultures they carry.

What does "Family Over Worries" mean?

FOW — Family Over Worries — is our mantra: the people who hold you together come before everything that weighs you down. It's embroidered on the sleeve of our pieces, and stitched into how the brand works.

Do I have to be a foreigner to wear FOREIGNERS?

No. This is bigger than where you were born. Wearing FOREIGNERS means you believe we are all human, whichever side of a border we come from. Warning: compliments may occur.

Why the bird?

Our symbol is the swift — a bird that spends its life in flight and crosses borders without ever asking permission. Wherever it lands, it is home. Nobody asks the swift where it's really from.

The 50%
Where does the 50% actually go?

On every piece sold, half of the profits go to organizations that support people who just arrived: language classes, paperwork, housing, a first job, and projects that keep their cultures alive. Partner organizations are announced with every drop and chosen with the community.

How do I know you actually donate?

After every drop, we publish the amount donated — down to the cent — along with the name of the partner organization. No fine print, no vague percentages.
